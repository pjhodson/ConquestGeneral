game overview

introduction

The world has been mostly covered with water as global warming has melted the polar ice caps. You are on a mission for your country to take what remains of the dry land. It seems the other countries want the highly contested land as well… Do whatever you deem necessary to make this a reality.

Conquest General is a Turn Based Strategy game, set in a near-apocalyptic future where the polar ice caps have melted due to global warming. Mankind does what it does best and fights over the few remaining resources.

You and your sworn enemy, therefore, are at WAR.

aesthetic goals

As you and your enemies are at WAR, competition is our main aesthetic goal. The thrill of building up an army and executing a grand master strategy which will allow you to watch your enemies burn should be reason enough for this game to be fun.

Assuming your enemy isn’t completely incompetent, there will also be elements of challenge involved.

There may also be some sensation involved, depending on the art and sound assets we end up incorporating in the final build of the game.

dynamics

We anticipate users will use one of several types of strategies while playing CONQUEST GENERAL. We expect users to either play as a rusher, a balanced player, or a turtle.

A typical rusher will build a vast quantity of low cost units quickly, and rush to the opponent, hoping that his sheer numbers will outperform whatever the opponent has managed to build thus far.

A balanced player will build low cost units at the beginning, and also have long term goals in mind. He or she may choose to build some larger units and have a diversified army rather than building a lot of the cheapest units and hurling them all at his or her enemy.

A turtle will sit in his base, much like a turtle sits in its shell, and build big units until he or she feels that there is no force under the sun that could possibly defeat his or her army. Once he or she has reached that point, he or she sets about defeating the enemy.

These play styles often conflict and will result in a large amount of challenge and competition. We anticipate the ability to play well and defeat enemies using any of these play styles will make CONQUEST GENERAL a lot of fun and also give it replay value.

mechanics

gameplay

game screens

There are 3 main screens in the game: The main game screen, the base screen, and the combat screen.

base screen

You have to manage your base, at which you construct buildings and train units.

These units can then be picked up by your Generals to roam the game board looking for power ups and enemies.

The General has to be on base to pick up troops!

main screen

Generals move from square to square, with the ability to pick up power ups to make their armies more powerful.

combat board

The combat board is where all combat will take place. Units have set movement and attack ranges. Players will take turns moving and attacking. Pictured is an impassable forest; terrain will have randomly generated obstacles to increase the strategy in combat.

the general

The General roams the game screen, looking for enemies, with the ultimate goal of destroying the enemy base. He takes with him TEN (10) units as his army. These units need not clutter up the main game screen, however, as they are used in combat only. the base

Players each have a base. At this base, they can create buildings and train troops. The training time for each unit is listed below, as is the building time for each building. If the general has his army of TEN (10) troops or is not at the base (touching one of the hexes occupied by the base) then up to TWENTY (20) troops trained will wait at the base until the general returns for reinforcements. starting the game

Players start the game with 28 points with which they can build a starting army. Players can choose units whose total hit points do not exceed 28 points. We limit 1 airplane unit per starting army.

Example:

Player A (Rusher) decides to start with as many low cost units as they can. They choose to start off with 7 grunts. Grunts have 4 hit points each, so player A uses all of his starting army points.

Player B (Balanced/Turtle) decides to start off with two tanks which are 10 HP each, and one airplane (8hp). They use all of their starting army points also.

A player cannot spend more than their starting army points. Any remaining points are discarded. taking a turn

Each player takes turns to do various things. When not in combat, the game is played on the main game screen. The player controls his or her general in movement around the game screen. Combat is initiated when a general moves into the square of the opposing player’s general. In a turn, a player may start building ONE (1) building, start training a unit at each building he or she already owns, and move the general on the main board. this may or may not lead to combat. Exiting combat immediately ends the attacking player’s turn, so plan carefully. Once a player’s turn is over, the other player takes his or her turn.

building a building

Buildings are built at bases. They produce units. They take some time to build.

barracks

time to build: 2 turns. produces: Grunts.

factory

time to build: 3 turns. produces: Tanks.

hangar

time to build: 5 turns. produces: Airplanes.

combat

Every unit gets a movement and an attack per turn. A unit does not have to move nor does it have to attack.

Combat is won when all enemy units are killed (the General cannot be killed in combat) or if the enemy successfully rolls a retreat check ( 8 or higher on a 1d12 roll )

The defeated general is left to rally his troops (if he has any) or return to base to reinforce or get ready for the final showdown.

The final showdown occurs when an enemy general enters a square occupied by the other player’s base. Final showdown is the same as any normal combat, except the defending player can use all troops stored at the base in the battle. There is also a base unit on the hex grid which has no attack or movement, and has 30 HP. If the defender’s base is destroyed, he or she loses, the game is over, and the attacking player wins.

unit ranges and combat statistics

grunt

4 HP Movement - 2 hex Train time: 2 turns Attack (ground only): Ranged (2 hex range): 1d4 Melee (adjacent): 1d6

tank

10 HP Movement - 3 hex Train time: 5 turns Attack (ground or air): Ranged (ground) (3 hex range): 1d10 Ranged (air) (3 hex range): 1d6

airplane

8HP Movement - 5 hex Train time: 7 turns Attack(ground or air) Ranged (air) (3 hex range): 1d6 Ground (occupied hex): 1d12; Splash Damage(adjacent hex): 1d4 Kamikaze attack (any hex), sacrifice plane: 1d20

sample combat

The players arrange the units their general had with him on the combat screen.

The attacking player then has a move and an attack action per unit. He or she does not have to move or attack with any units.

If the opposing general is a lily-livered yellow belly, he or she can roll a 1d12 retreat check. 8 or higher is a successful retreat and they exit combat and get to move 3 spaces on the main game screen.

Players take turns moving and attacking with their units until all of one player’s units are destroyed or a player successfully retreats.

winning the game When a player gets a general to the other player’s base a final battle will take place

The defending player can fight with every unit stored within his base

The base has 30 HP and does occupy a spot on the combat grid unlike a general

If the attacking player destroys the base the game is over!

user interface

main menu

The main menu greets the user upon launch. It will be fairly simple, as Unity takes care of all of the graphics options and resolution before the game is launched. As such, our main menu will contain our game’s logo, a play button, and an exit button. The right half of the picture here shows desired hover feedback for the user. A 3D effect will be applied on click.

building selection at base

(e.g. which building would you like to build at this spot?) When clicking on an open plot of land within the base board the player will have a popup menu to select which building they would like to build on that plot of land.

unit selection from building

(e.g. click barracks sel3ect grunt) Clicking on an already built building will popup a unit build menu allowing the player to select which unit to build in that building.

unit stats & selection

In the battle board when a player hovers over a unit they will have a display of the stats (movement, damage, health) of that unit. Also this unit will be selected for movement when clicked on.

attack selector (Ranged/Melee Land/Air Normal/Kamikaze)

Highlighted area when a unit is told to attack, showing the range of the different attacks it can perform.

turn counter

Some counter on the bottom of the game counting the turns that go by.

player turn indicator

Some kind of indication letting the player know that it is their turn, e.g. “RED TURN” or “BLUE TURN” grid overlays (regular + hex) Showing the borders of the hexes on the field.

tutorial popups (time permitting) (if enabled)

Players will have the option to turn on or off the tutorial within the in game options menu. (It is important to note that there is no options menu from the main menu.) When enabled the game will display popups with helpful tips on how to play the game. How to build / move units, how combat works… ect. These popups will only come up 1 time unless reenabled within the options menu.

build queue (time permitting)

From the main board and the base board the player will have a sidebar informing him of current build times for units and buildings.

scripts

Daniel Reed and Daniel Zhao will be responsible for the following scripts:

main board

general at base?

If the general is at the base, user can add troops to general in the Base screen. This script will therefore need to interact with the Base screen.

general movement

This script will control the generals’ movements throughout the world, checking for invalid moves and correcting them post haste.

powerUp pickup

This will be a check to see if the player has picked up a powerup. The powerups will interact with mostly the combat board but might interact with all boards.

player turns

This script will handle who’s turn it is. It will need to be accessed by all scripts.

unit collision

This will be used to detect if a general has collided with another general or a base.

user interface

This script will handle all of the UI elements on the main board.

combat board

unit placement

Players will be able to place units on any valid hex on their half of the board.

unit movement

Units will only be able to move within their set limits. This script will define those limits and perform the movements.

damage calculation & health calculation

This script will calculate dice rolls for damage and affect the health of attacked units.

particles for explosions

We will want to instantiate particle effects on units that explode. This might not be a separate script, but part of damage calculation.

random terrain (impassible land)

Generates a random combat board with impassible terrain or other obstacles on the map.

player turns

This script will determine whose turn it is. This will be confined to the combat board only.

user interface

This script will handle all UI elements on the combat board.

bases

unit build queues

This script will contain a list of units being built, and will tick down as turns progress, creating a unit once its train time has depleted. This script will also make sure players cannot train more than one unit at a given building. This script will probably be applied to building prefabs.

building build queues

This script is similar to the Unit Build queue, but for buildings. This will probably be applied to possible building squares, and will only allow for one building per square.

units at base

This script will store the units that are built already but not currently with a general.

units on general (if general at base)

If this script is told that the general is at the base, it will allow players to drag units from the base reserve onto the general.

UI for transfer units from base to general

This script will handle moving a unit from the reserve area in the base to a spot with a general.
